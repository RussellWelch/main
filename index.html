Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting electronic information by mitigating information risks and vulnerabilities. Information risks can include unauthorized access, use, disclosure, interception, or destruction of data. Data can include, but is not limited to, the confidential information of business or individual users.

Vulnerabilities are weaknesses in systems or processes that can be exploited to gain unauthorized access to data or resources. Common cybersecurity vulnerabilities include weak passwords, unpatched software, and insecure network architecture.

To mitigate these risks, cybersecurity practitioners implement security controls such as access control lists and firewalls. They also develop policies and procedures for secure handling of data. In addition, they may provide user training on proper security practices.
